# Code of conduct

## Our standard

We are committed to a respectful, technically rigorous, and welcoming project.
Participants should:

- discuss evidence and behavior rather than attacking people;
- welcome questions and corrections from different disciplines and experience levels;
- state uncertainty, calibration assumptions, and verification limits clearly;
- respect privacy, licensing, attribution, and responsible disclosure; and
- accept constructive feedback and help repair harm when mistakes occur.

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized conduct, deliberate
misrepresentation, disclosure of private information, and sustained disruption
are not acceptable.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may edit or remove contributions, comments, or other
participation that violates this standard, and may temporarily or permanently
restrict participation. Enforcement decisions should consider context, impact,
and a proportionate opportunity to correct behavior.

Report conduct concerns privately through the repository maintainer when
possible. If no private channel is available, open a minimal issue requesting
private contact without including sensitive details. Maintainers will protect
reporter privacy as far as practical and will not knowingly retaliate against a
good-faith report.

This policy applies in repository spaces and whenever someone officially
represents the project.
